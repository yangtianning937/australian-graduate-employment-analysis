# Executive Summary

## Project Overview

This project analyses Australian graduate employment outcomes using real public data from the QILT Graduate Outcomes Survey - Longitudinal (GOS-L) 2025 National Report Tables. The focus is Computing and Information Systems (CIS), with comparisons across other study areas and universities.

The goal is to understand whether CIS graduates show strong medium-term employment and salary outcomes, and whether gender pay differences remain visible in this field.

## Key Metrics for CIS Graduates

| Metric | Result |
|---|---:|
| Medium-term full-time employment rate | 91.0% |
| Medium-term overall employment rate | 92.0% |
| Short-term median salary | AUD 69,100 |
| Medium-term median salary | AUD 100,000 |
| Salary growth | AUD 30,900 |
| Male medium-term median salary | AUD 103,000 |
| Female medium-term median salary | AUD 94,000 |
| Gender pay gap | 8.7% |

## Main Insights

1. CIS graduates have strong medium-term outcomes.

CIS graduates reached a 91.0% medium-term full-time employment rate and a 92.0% medium-term overall employment rate. This suggests that CIS is one of the stronger study areas for employment outcomes.

2. CIS salary outcomes are competitive.

The CIS medium-term median salary is AUD 100,000, with salary growth of AUD 30,900 from short-term to medium-term. This indicates strong salary progression after graduation.

3. A gender pay gap still exists in CIS.

Male CIS graduates had a medium-term median salary of AUD 103,000, while female CIS graduates had AUD 94,000. The calculated gender pay gap is about 8.7%.

4. Aggregated data supports benchmarking, not causal claims.

The QILT public workbook provides summary-level data. It is useful for comparing study areas and universities, but it does not support individual-level causal analysis.
